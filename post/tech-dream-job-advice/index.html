Make Your Tech Dream Job A Reality

March 19, 2016

Here’s some advice that was given to me a few years back. It takes considerable time (at least a year) and effort, but it worked for me. The advice holds true if you are unemployed, or gainfully employed but not working with the technologies you want to be.

Build things you want to see in the world

Pick a language or platform that you’re interested in working with and start making things. Whether its hardware, embedded, web, mobile, desktop, or augmented reality start building and keep building.

If you don’t know what you want to see in the world find a non-profit and see if they need any tech work done. They’ll be thrilled to have help. Its a real world problem and your work will get more exposure than it would get on its own. You’ll also start making personal connections with people (see point #3).

People don’t get jobs through their resumes they get jobs through people

You can apply and apply to hundreds of job postings for months and months and not get anywhere. But, as soon as you know the hiring manager you’ll get an interview faster than you thought possible.

Go to meetups. Yes this takes time and effort. No you won’t meet people the first time you go, but after a while you’ll start to recognize faces and people will recognize you.

Most meetups are just fronts to recruit people for jobs. You get to meet the people that will hire you and get a feel for the place you may work at. There’s less stress than an interview and your judgement won’t be as clouded.

Go out with everyone after the meetup is over. The talk is just a precursor, the main event is the socializing that takes place afterwards.

Share with others what you are doing

At a minimum you should be writing about the things you are learning and building on a blog. Post your resume/cv in plain text as a static page. Ensure that your blog is able to be indexed by search engines.

Additionally, if you choose to do so post your code on GitHub. In many ways, your GitHub profile is your professional portfolio. If you don’t like the idea of open sourcing, build things and sell them. That way you have something to show that demonstrates your skills, and may even make you money.

Finally once you’ve been making amazing things you want to see in the world and sharing your knowledge with others, ask to start speaking at meetups. You’ll be seen as a thought leader and highly hireable.

And, remember most people miss opportunities because they’re disguised as work.
